Research Areas

Interpersonal Perception

This work examines how we perceive information about other people. This ranges from their current internal states (i.e., emotions, beliefs, intentions) to enduring semantic and relational knowledge about them (i.e., intrapersonal knowledge such as personality, and interpersonal knowledge such as relationships).

Learning & Decision-Making

This work explores how we update information about other people and subsequently act in ways that affect them. This includes learning how specific actions affect outcomes in social contexts, assigning value to others' welfare, preferences, or goals, and inferring others' representations of the world through observation.

Shared/Vicarious Experience

This work examines how dynamic social interactions shape our own experiences. This includes how other people influence our own outcomes, behaviors, or internal states, how our behaviors and neurophysiology align with others' behaviors and neurophysiology, and how we empathize for others' emotions.

Mental Health & Well-Being

This work disentangles the societal, behavioral, and neurobiological factors that promote human welfare using computational tools (e.g., modeling). This includes examining variation in these phenomena to inform public policy and/or advance the prevention, diagnosis, and treatment of psychiatric conditions.

Georgetown Methods Lab

I started Georgetown Methods Lab, which is a trainee-run intiative established for the purpose of providing a space beyond the classroom to exchange ideas, share code, receive feedback, learn modern research methods, and foster a culture of open science in psychological science and cognitive neuroscience at Georgetown University. Email methodslab@georgetown.edu to get involved!

LSAN tools

I currently maintain lsan_tools, which is a Python package (in-development) built to streamline behavioral and neuroimaging data manipulation and analysis in the Laboratory on Social and Affective Neuroscience at Georgetown University.
