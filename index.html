Shawn Rhoads

National Science Foundation Graduate Research Fellow

I am a PhD student in the Laboratory on Social and Affective Neuroscience at Georgetown University conducting research on human prosocial behavior and its underlying mechanisms (e.g., empathy, shared affective experiences, interpersonal perception) under the mentorship of Dr. Abigail Marsh. I graduated from the University of Southern California with a Bachelor's Degree in both physics and psychology. Currently, I work on projects pertaining to dyadic social perceptions, hyberbolic social discounting, and real-world extraordinary altruism. I am also an avid social justice, environmental, and open-science advocate. I have been involved with advocacy work for environmental sustainability and social justice on both the local and national level.
