ABOUT

Who I am

My name is Shawn Rhoads. I graduated from the University of Southern California with a Bachelors Degree in physics and psychology. My studies have led me to pursue my interests to conduct research on how the brain works. I have also been involved with advocacy work for environmental sustainability on both the local and national level.

My story

I began my USC undergraduate career as a physics major after transfering from Stevens Institute of Technology in 2012. I immediately developed an interest for research as an assistant under Dr. Edward Rhodes, studying helioseismology through analysis of data collected from the Mt. Wilson Observatory, the High Degree Heliosesimic Network, and the Stanford Joint Science Operations Center. To pacify my insatiable curiosity about the human mind, I added a second major of psychology and began exploring my interests from a neuroscientific perspective. I served as a research assistant at the USC Brain and Creativity Institute studying adolescents' social emotions under the guidance of Dr. Mary Helen Immordino-Yang. I currently work at the UC Davis Center for Mind and Brain as a Lab Manager under Drs. Amanda Guyer and Paul Hastings.

My future

I will be starting my journey towards a Ph.D. in Lifespan Cognitive Neuroscience at Georgetown University in the fall 2017. I will be working with Dr. Abigail Marsh and her Laboratory on Social and Affective Neuroscience to unpack the neural substrates of empathy and social cognition across a continuum of functioning. I hope to pursue this research in collaboration with policymakers, educators, physicians, business professionals and fellow scientists to help foster a more collective culture of empathy and illuminate how intersections of neurobiology, social context, culture and emotion can allow for greater conversations and understanding among people everywhere.

ENVIRONMENTAL WORK

Environmental sustainability and thrivability should be at the forefront of our world's minds. My advocacy work seeks to produce cultural shifts in the way people act and think about our natural world by fostering collaboration, innovation, and shared learning through the cultivation of ecosystems, biodiversity, and natural resources.

Roles and Accomplishments

Collaborated with students, faculty, and staff across varying departments to compose a comprehensive sustainability plan of action for the University of Southern California (USC News: 9/11/15, 10/23/15, 11/23/15; USC Daily Trojan: 4/14/15).

Pioneered a synchronous day of action with students on 70 college campuses across the nation to amplify a message to combat climate change (USC News: 9/29/15; Daily Trojan: 9/30/15, 10/4/15).

Served as a leading architect of the bylaws, allocation, and procedure for the distribution of $90,000 towards student-driven sustainability projects at USC; The USC Green Engagement Fund received a total of $169,816.01 grant requests during its first year. (USC Daily Trojan: 3/10/15).

Established USC Environmental Student Assembly under the Programming Branch of the Undergraduate Student Government as the Founding Executive Director to unite and serve as a resource to all students and organizations that seek to expand environmental action and awareness on campus (USC Daily Trojan: 12/4/13; 9/9/14, 4/1/15).

Represented voice of the environmental student community at USC through active response to current affairs, communication with University administration, and advancement of University initiatives (USC Daily Trojan: 10/20/14, 10/30/14, 8/31/15; Neon Tommy: 4/2/15, 5/24/15; USC Annenberg TV News: 4/2/15; Inside Higher Ed: 4/16/15).

Partnered with local, environmentally-focused community organizations to support their initiatives on and off campus (USC News: 3/10/15, 9/28/15; USC Daily Trojan: 10/3/14, 1/27/15, 2/23/15, 3/9/15).

Transformed an initial budget of $5000 into spending of $50,000 across 85 original large-scale sustainability events and programs over two years for the entire student body (USC News: 4/3/14; Daily Trojan: 4/22/14, 10/16/14, 10/16/14, 11/23/14, 4/5/15, 11/5/15).
