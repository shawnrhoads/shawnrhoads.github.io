ABOUT

Who I am

My name is Shawn Rhoads. I graduated from the University of Southern California with a Bachelors Degree in both physics and psychology. My studies have led me to pursue my interests to conduct research on the empathic brain. I have also been involved with advocacy work for environmental sustainability and social justice on both the local and national level.

My story

As a physics student at USC, I immediately developed a profound interest in research as an assistant under Dr. Edward Rhodes using solar imaging data collected at Mt. Wilson Observatory to study helioseismology (the sun's sound waves). To feed my curiosity about the human mind, I added a second major of psychology and began exploring my interests from a neuroscientific perspective. I served as a research assistant at the USC Brain and Creativity Institute studying adolescents' social emotions under the guidance of Dr. Mary Helen Immordino-Yang. I currently work at the UC Davis Center for Mind and Brain as lab manager under Drs. Amanda Guyer and Paul Hastings.

My future

I will be starting my journey towards a Ph.D. in Lifespan Cognitive Neuroscience at Georgetown University in the fall 2017. I will be working with Dr. Abigail Marsh and her Laboratory on Social and Affective Neuroscience to unpack the neural substrates of empathy, altruism and social cognition across a continuum of functioning. I hope to pursue this research in collaboration with policymakers, educators, physicians, business professionals and fellow scientists to help foster a more collective culture of empathy and illuminate how intersections of neurobiology, social context, culture and emotion can allow for greater conversations and understanding among people everywhere.

ENVIRONMENTAL WORK

Environmental sustainability and thrivability should be at the forefront of our world's minds. My advocacy work seeks to produce cultural shifts in the way people act and think about our natural world by fostering collaboration, innovation, and shared learning through the cultivation of ecosystems, biodiversity, and natural resources.
