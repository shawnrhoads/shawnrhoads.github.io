ABOUT

Who I am

My name is Shawn Rhoads. I am a Ph.D. student at Georgetown University interested in conducting neuroscientific research on human empathy, altruism, and social connection. I graduated from the University of Southern California with a Bachelor's Degree in both physics and psychology. I have also been involved with advocacy work for environmental sustainability and social justice on both the local and national level.

My story

As a physics student at USC, I immediately developed an interest in scientific research as an assistant under Dr. Edward Rhodes using solar imaging data to study helioseismology. I added a second major of psychology and began exploring my interests via neuroimaging methods. I served as a research assistant at the USC Brain and Creativity Institute studying adolescents' social emotions under the guidance of Dr. Mary Helen Immordino-Yang before working at the UC Davis Center for Mind and Brain as lab manager under Drs. Amanda Guyer and Paul Hastings.

My future

I am a National Science Foundation research fellow and Ph.D. student in Lifespan Cognitive Neuroscience at Georgetown University. I work with Dr. Abigail Marsh and her Laboratory on Social and Affective Neuroscience to unpack how the human brain encodes information about our social world; and how the neural mechanisms that sub-serve social cognitive and affective functioning (e.g., mentalizing, empathy) interact to promote costly prosocial decision-making and behavior towards close others and distant strangers.

ENVIRONMENTAL WORK

Environmental sustainability and thrivability should be at the forefront of our world's minds. My advocacy work seeks to produce cultural shifts in the way people act and think about our natural world by fostering collaboration, innovation, and shared learning through the cultivation of ecosystems, biodiversity, and natural resources.
